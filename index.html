Phylum: Chordata | Class: Reptilia | Family: Agamidae

Hygroscopic Water Transport

One of the most significant adaptations of the Thorny Devil is its ability to harvest moisture from arid environments. The skin surface is micro-structured with semi-tubular channels between overlapping scales. These channels act as capillaries, pulling water from damp sand or morning dew against the force of gravity directly toward the buccal cavity (mouth).

Convergent Evolution

The Thorny Devil is a classic example of convergent evolution. While it looks remarkably similar to the North American Horned Lizard (Phrynosoma), they are only distantly related. Both have independently evolved spiny armor and ant-specialized diets to thrive in similar desert niches.

Quick Stats:
  • Diet: Obligate Myrmecophagy (Ants)
  • Max Length: 21 cm (8.3 in)
  • Longevity: 15-20 years
  • Habitat: Great Sandy Desert, AU

Note: A single adult can consume up to 2,500 ants in a single feeding session.
